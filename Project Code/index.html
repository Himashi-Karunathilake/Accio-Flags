What is Accio Flags?

Harry Potter along with his friends Ron and Hermione go through various challenges in order to destroy the evil dark wizard, Lord Voldermot. To defeat him, Harry has to find and destroy 7 Hocruxes. A Horcrux is an object formed by dark magic that is used by a wizard or a witch to achieve immortality by splitting their soul into separate pieces.

We created this CTF based on the 7 Hocruxes. Your mission is to collect all 7 Hocruxes in order to defeat Lord Voldermot.

As you progress through this journey, don't forget to save the flags and other valuable information that you collect in a text document!!! It will make this journey easier for you...

Please note that backward navigation is disabled and if tried, you will have to restart the challenge!!!



About Us

What is our goal?

Our goal is to give you a chance to experience what information security is, first hand. This CTF was made for cybersecurity intern recruiters to check the practical ability of interns before rectruitment. We expect that this will be a much more efficient way of recruitment than asking theoretical questions. We want to give practical knowledge instead of black-and-white knowledge.

If you are an undergraduate student interested in a career in information security, this CTF is ideal for you too. We have created this by including common information security and computer science principles.

There are a growing number of information security degrees offered by universities. However, students don’t always understand what “Information Security” is before joining these degree programs and they often ask their peers what information security exactly means and what information security professionals do. Our aim is to target undergraduate students who have an interest in having a career in the field of information security and who are interested in specializing in cybersecurity. By participating in this CTF they will be able to get a clear understanding on the principles of information security and the methods and techniques used. It provides them a way to have fun while gathering a vast amount of new knowledge.

Students are free to use any resource they want while they attempt the CTF. We believe that while trying to find a way to find the flags, they will be able to explore interesting areas in cybersecurity. Although we target undergraduate students, this CTF is suitable for anyone interested in the fascinating field of cybersecurity.
